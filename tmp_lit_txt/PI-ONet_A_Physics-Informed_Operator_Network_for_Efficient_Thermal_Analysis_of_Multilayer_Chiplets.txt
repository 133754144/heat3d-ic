                              III. PI-ONET FRAMEWORK
                The PI-ONet proposed in this work is a deep learning
             framework that combines PINN and data-driven approaches to
             predict the temperature distribution in chiplets. To maximize
             practicability, the chiplets are allowed to have the following
             characteristics: 1) a multilayer structure with varying thickness
             and materials; 2) TSV and bump layers that contain TSV
             and bump arrays of diﬀerent sizes and densities; and 3) input
             power maps that allow spatial distribution to mimic various
             working conditions in reality. The special techniques and
             treatments that we devise to enable these modeling capabilities
             are detailed in Sections III-A–III-F.

                                                                                                                                  VI. CONCLUSION
                                                                                                         This study introduces a PI-ONet designed for eﬃcient
                                                                                                     steady-state thermal modeling of multilayer chiplets. By
                                                                                                     integrating physical constraints into the learning process,
                                                                                                     PI-ONet eﬀectively maps power distribution to temperature
                                                                                                     distribution within chiplet structures with limited training
                                                                                                     data. In contrast to existing neural network models for
                                                                                                     thermal modeling, PI-ONet incorporates two key works: a
                                                                                                     thermal extraction module that simpliﬁes the modeling of
                                                                                                     TSV and bump layers, and an enhanced DeepONet architec-
                                                                                                     ture that blends physics-based and data-driven loss functions.
                                                                                                     These enhancements enable PI-ONet to handle chiplets with
                                                                                                     industrial-grade complexity. Experimental results demonstrate
                                                                                                     that PI-ONet not only achieves higher accuracy and robust-
                                                                                                     ness compared to existing PINNs but also operates several
                                                                                                     orders of magnitude faster than traditional FEM simula-
                                                                                                     tions. Future research will focus on extending PI-ONet from
                                                                                                     steady-state to transient thermal analysis, allowing the model
                                                                                                     to track and predict real-time temperature variations under
                                                                                                     dynamic power ﬂuctuations. This extension will support more
                                                                                                     advanced thermal management strategies for chiplets, particu-
                                                                                                     larly in scenarios involving rapid power changes. In addition,
                                                                                                     future work will explore nonuniform discretization strategies
                                                                                                     to better balance spatial resolution against computational
                                                                                                     eﬃciency.
