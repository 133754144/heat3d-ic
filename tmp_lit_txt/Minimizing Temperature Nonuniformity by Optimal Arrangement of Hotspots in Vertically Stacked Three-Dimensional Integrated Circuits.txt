                    Generally, heat sinks are designed assuming that a uniform heat
                 ﬂux is produced at the top surface of a microprocessors (convec-
                 tive surface) and the performance of the heat sink is evaluated in
                 terms of the thermal resistance (one-dimensional) or maximum
                 temperature rise. For efﬁcient thermal management of microchips
                 with multiple heat sources, uniform temperature distribution is
                 preferred on the heat sink surface.
                    It is evident from the literature that one of the major challenges
                 in designing an efﬁcient heat sink is to minimize the temperature
                 nonuniformity on a microprocessor surface caused by heterogene-
                 ous diverse power distribution. This work in this paper is not
                 motivated by the necessity to develop thermal solutions which can
                 maintain uniform temperature distribution at the heat sink’s sur-
                 face, rather to ﬁnd optimal arrangement of the heat sources which
                 provide a uniform temperature distribution in a double-layered
                 3D-IC system. Furthermore, the ﬂoor planning technique for 3D
                 ICs available in literature lacks a mathematical model or a regres-
                 sion equation to characterize the location of the heat sources.
                 Also, the nonoverlapping constraint is not imposed so efﬁciently
                 in the optimization problem available in literature. This work
                 aims to efﬁciently impose and characterize the nonoverlapping
                 constraint in the optimization problem by deﬁning a unique geo-
                 metrical parameter and obtain solutions using a stochastic optimi-
                 zation algorithm. An analytical–numerical model coupled with                  Fig. 1  Heattransferdescriptionoftheproblem
